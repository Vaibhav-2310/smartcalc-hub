What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple measurement that compares your weight with your height to estimate whether your body weight falls within a healthy range. BMI is widely used by doctors and health organizations as a screening tool for weight-related health risks.

Understanding BMI Categories

BMI categories are based on recommendations from health organizations. BMI is calculated using only two measurements:

  • Weight in kilograms (kg)
  • Height in meters (m)
  • Below 18.5 – Underweight: Your body weight is lower than the recommended range for your height. Improving nutrition and gaining healthy weight may be beneficial.
  • 18.5 to 24.9 – Normal Weight: Your weight is considered healthy for your height. Maintaining regular exercise and a balanced diet is recommended.
  • 25 to 29.9 – Overweight: Your body weight is above the recommended range for your height. Increasing physical activity and improving eating habits may help.
  • 30 and Above – Obese: A BMI above 30 may increase the risk of conditions such as diabetes, high blood pressure and heart disease. This refers to BMI above 30 and does not mean body weight above 30 kilograms.

How is BMI Calculated?

BMI is calculated using the following formula:

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)

For example, if a person weighs 70 kg and their height is 1.75 m:

BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.86

A BMI of 22.86 falls within the Normal Weight category.

Example BMI Calculation

Suppose a person is:

  • Age: 32 years
  • Weight: 59 kg
  • Height: 5 feet 4 inches (1.6256 meters)

Their BMI would be:

BMI = 59 ÷ (1.6256 × 1.6256) ≈ 22.3

A BMI of approximately 22.3 falls under the Normal Weight category. This example shows that BMI depends on both height and weight together and not on body weight alone.

Does Age Affect BMI?

For adults aged 18 years and above, BMI categories remain the same regardless of age or gender. BMI uses only height and weight to estimate a healthy weight range.

For children and teenagers below 18 years, BMI interpretation is different and also considers age and gender.
